## [LLM Roadmap](https://github.com/AlleninTaipei/Artificial-Intelligence-Study/blob/main/LLM%20Roadmap.md)

The LLM roadmap is divided into three main sections: LLM Fundamentals, LLM Scientist, and LLM Engineer.
Each section covers essential topics and skills necessary for understanding, developing, and deploying large language models (LLMs).

|LLM|Key characteristics|
|-|-|
|Size and Scale|LLMs have billions of parameters. For example, GPT-3, an LLM developed by OpenAI, has 175 billion parameters.|
|Training Data|They are trained on diverse datasets that include text from books, websites, articles, and other written content available on the internet.|
|Capabilities|LLMs can perform a variety of language tasks, such as answering questions, summarizing text, translating languages, generating creative content, and even engaging in conversation.|
|Applications|They are used in numerous applications, including chatbots, virtual assistants, automated content creation, language translation, and more. Large Language Models, like the one you are interacting with (ChatGPT), are designed to generate human-like text based on the input they receive, making them useful for a wide range of natural language processing tasks.|

|LLM IP|"Large Language Model Intellectual Property.", this encompasses the proprietary aspects of large language models developed by organizations.|
|-|-|
|Algorithms and Architectures|The specific design and implementation of the neural network architecture used in the model, including any innovative techniques or optimizations.|
|Training Data|The curated datasets used to train the model, which may be proprietary or uniquely compiled to improve the model's performance.|
|Model Weights|The trained parameters of the model, which are the result of extensive computational resources and time spent training the model.|
|Training Techniques|Proprietary methods and techniques used to train the model, including data preprocessing, augmentation, and optimization strategies.|
|Applications and Use Cases|The specific applications or products built using the LLM, including software that integrates the model for various tasks.|
|Patents and Trademarks|Any patents filed related to the technology, processes, or applications of the LLM, as well as trademarks associated with the brand or specific implementations of the model.|
